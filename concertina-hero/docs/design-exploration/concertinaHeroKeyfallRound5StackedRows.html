Stacked rows, done right

Corrected: on a real anglo the top and bottom rows sit in the same columns, one above the other, and only the middle row is offset. So a column's two buttons share a lane — and that is exactly the case to design for.

The corrected layout

Ten lanes, wider notes

Because the top and bottom rows share a column, there are ten lanes per hand, not fifteen — five column lanes plus five offset lanes for the middle row. Wider lanes mean the note can carry its letter again, not just a colour. Shade still tells the row apart: dark is the top row, light is the bottom.

Your question · two notes on a stacked pair

When the shared lane has two notes

A top button and the bottom button beneath it share one column lane. What happens depends entirely on whether they sound together.

1 At different times

They simply queue in the lane, one above the other. Shade says which row; only the near one lights its key. No conflict — different beats never overlap.

2 At the same time

They would cover each other, so on that beat they split — top slides left, bottom slides right. Both keys light. It reads as the two-finger press it is.

Dark — top row
Light — bottom row
Same hue = same column; the shade separates the stack.

Notes

What this settles

  • The stack is a two-way call, not a guess. A shared lane only ever holds the top or the bottom button of one column, and shade plus the lit key name which — far simpler than the five-column grid's three-way guess.
  • The split only fires when it must. Notes stay centred on the lane and land true; the left/right slide happens only on a beat where a stacked pair sounds together, so the layout stays stable the rest of the time.
  • Ten lanes buys width back. The earlier fifteen-lane fear came from the wrong (sheared) layout. With the real stacking, notes are wide enough to keep their letter.
  • Open: the middle row's offset lanes fall between the column lanes — should a middle-row note read as clearly "between" two columns, or do we want a subtle marker so it isn't mistaken for a column note?
