Held notes how a hold plays

A note's height already is its length — a long note is a tall bar. Playing one runs in three beats of motion: press when the head reaches the line, keep the key down as the bar feeds through it, release as the tail clears. The button stays lit the whole way, and points build for the fraction you actually hold.

1 Approach

The tall bar is the hold. Its rounded head is the moment to press — three beats long here.

2 Hold

Head past the line, key down. The bar feeds through and shrinks; the button glows as points build.

3 Release

The tail reaches the line — let go. A clean full hold banks the note's full value.

Let go early and the bar stops feeding: you keep the points earned so far, but not the rest. Same on every instrument size.
